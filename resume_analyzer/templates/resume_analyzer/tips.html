ATS Resume Tips

Format & Structure

  • Use standard fonts: Stick to Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman (10-12pt)
  • Clear section headers: Use standard headings like "Work Experience", "Education", "Skills"
  • Avoid tables and graphics: Use simple bullet points and text formatting
  • Save as .docx or .pdf: These formats are most ATS-friendly

Keywords & Content

  • Mirror job descriptions: Use keywords from the job posting naturally
  • Include industry terms: Use relevant technical skills and buzzwords
  • Spell out acronyms: Use both "Search Engine Optimization (SEO)" and "SEO"
  • Use action verbs: Start bullet points with strong action words

Contact & Personal Info

  • Include full contact info: Name, phone, email, city/state, LinkedIn
  • Use a professional email: firstname.lastname@email.com format
  • Avoid photos: Unless specifically required, don't include headshots
  • Skip personal details: Age, marital status, etc. are not ATS-friendly

Common Mistakes

  • Headers and footers: Important info can be missed in these sections
  • Special characters: Avoid excessive symbols, boxes, or unusual formatting
  • Multiple columns: Can confuse ATS parsing - stick to single column layout
  • Creative templates: Fancy designs often don't parse well

Optimization Tips

  • Tailor for each job: Customize your resume for each application
  • Quantify achievements: Use numbers and percentages where possible
  • Keep it relevant: Focus on experiences related to the target role
  • Test your resume: Use tools like this one to check ATS compatibility
